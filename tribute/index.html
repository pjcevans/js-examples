Grace Hopper

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper

Inventer of the first compiler for a computer programming language

Hopper was one of those rare visionaries who could understand the potential of something well before her peers.

"I had a running compiler and nobody would touch it. They told me computers could only do arithmetic."

Grace Hopper

As well as being an inspiring individual Grace Hopper made the progress she did despite living in a time period plagued by patriarchal inequality. Hopper succeded in an industry, which still suffers from a lack of diversity in places, that was accustomed to the success of men - exemplified by her being awarded the inaugural Data Processing Management Association Man of the Year award

Hopper also excelled in another industry at that time dominated by the opposite gender by becoming a Rear admiral in the United States Navy

The USS Hopper, named for the pioneering computer scientist Rear Admiral "Amazing" Grace Hopper.

"The most important thing I've accomplished, other than building the compiler, is training young people. They come to me, you know, and say, 'Do you think we can do this?' I say, "Try it." And I back 'em up. They need that. I keep track of them as they get older and I stir 'em up at intervals so they don't forget to take chances."

Grace Hopper
